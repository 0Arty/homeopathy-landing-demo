What is homeopathy?

Homeopathy is a gentle, holistic medicine that treats the whole person, not just the illness. The person, the symptoms, the disease—and how you experience them—all matter. By understanding this full picture, treatment is truly individual.

How It Works

The core principle of homeopathy is “like cures like.” A substance that can cause certain symptoms in a healthy person can help relieve those same symptoms in someone unwell. For example, “hair of the dog” for a hangover follows this idea—the same thing that caused it, in a small dose, can ease it. Another example is the onion: it can make your eyes water and nose run, and in homeopathy, a remedy made from onion may be used to support someone with hay fever showing those same symptoms.

More Than One Principle

Homeopathy isn’t just about similarity. Remedies are prepared and given in the minimum dose. They are also chosen according to the meaningful totality of symptoms and tailored to the individual. Together, these four principles—similarity, minimum dose, totality, and individuality—make homeopathy a complete system of healing.
